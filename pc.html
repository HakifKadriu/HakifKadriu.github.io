Gaming PCs Explained

BY HAKIF KADRIU

Whats a gaming pc?

A gaming computer, also known as a gaming PC, is a specialized personal computer designed for playing video games at very high standards. Although available ready built, gaming PCs are often custom made for the serious enthusiast. They have as much as 32GB of RAM and the fastest CPU and GPU chips that are generally no more than one generation behind. Typically using a tower case to accommodate multiple drives and state-of-the-art graphics cards that can cost five times as much as an entire non-gaming PC, such machines often use elaborate cooling mechanisms, especially if the CPU is overclocked.

OMEN GAMING PC

Founded in Canada, then bought by HP in 2005, Omen is one of the greatest companies of Gaming PCs up to this day. Omen is a highly rated tech company that began very small and was known as Voodoo PC before being acquired by HP.

HP is known for producing high-quality computer products. The Omen division uses resources and technology to create some of the greatest gaming PCs available.

Omen now includes mice, headsets, and keyboards in the company's characteristic black with red accents color scheme. Its current top-of-the-line PC, the Omen Obelisk, has outstanding performance.

ALIENWARE GAMING PC

Dell acquired Alienware in 2006, and the company has since continued to produce high-quality gaming hardware. The company is known for pushing the limits of what a gaming computer can be.

Many of its designs are unique to the market, but the quality is always excellent. Alienware is still at the top of its game for gaming PCs, as evidenced by the new Aurora R11 Gaming Desktop.

This is undoubtedly one of the best gaming PC brands. But it comes second after Omen due to their higher prices.

CORSAIR GAMING PC

The software provides real-time status for all of your components, allowing you to sync them to function better together and have matching RGB lighting colors. The Corsair Vengeance i7200 is the company's latest gaming PC that performs like a full-time gaming machine.

Although the Corsair Vengeance i7200 is not as expensive as some of its competitors, it still provides access to some of the most powerful hardware available.

Because the Vengeance i7200 comes in a plain-looking casing with no peripherals, this is the case. However, if you value substance over style, this is the gaming PC for you.

You can build a beast of a machine with CPUs up to an Intel Core i9-10850K and GPUs up to an Nvidia GeForce RTX 3090 if you can afford it.

ORIGIN PC

Origin PC is currently a Corsair subsidiary. However, we've kept it on the list since it provides a distinct service from its parent company. Origin PC was founded in 2009 by former Alienware employees who wished to return to custom PC development.

Rather than spending hours assembling a one-of-a-kind rig, you can hire Origin PC to do it for you. Everything about the case, from the color to the material used for the panels, can be customized.

You should expect to pay more for an Origin PC build than you would for a conventional prebuilt PC, but it's well worth it for a one-of-a-kind machine.
